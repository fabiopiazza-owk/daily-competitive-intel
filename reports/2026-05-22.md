## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 Active Evidence-Seeking Layer for K Clinical Agents
**Rationale:** ClinSeekAgent crystallizes a capability gap — real clinical/biomarker workflows require agents that iteratively *plan and retrieve* multimodal evidence (pathology, omics, notes, literature), not consume pre-curated context. This is the new agentic baseline and directly differentiates K from generic OpenAI deployments hitting pharma.
**Effort:** Large (1–2 quarters) — requires retrieval orchestration, multimodal tool routing, and audit logging for GxP.

### 🥈 Indication-Specific Foundation Model Co-Build Offering (Pharma-Branded)
**Rationale:** Ksana's $17.9M behavioral-health LLM with Providence shows funders and partners want *co-developed* vertical models, not off-the-shelf. Packaging a co-build SKU for AstraZeneca/Sanofi (e.g., oncology multimodal FM with partner data) defends against OpenAI horizontal entry and locks in strategic accounts.
**Effort:** Medium-Large — productize what K already does bespoke; needs commercial model + data governance template.

### 🥉 EMA/FDA Divergence Module for Biomarker Evidence Packages
**Rationale:** Today's AstraZeneca breast cancer EU/US advisor split is a recurring pattern. A K capability that auto-generates region-specific sub-population evidence and responder analyses gives pharma a concrete weapon for transatlantic submissions — directly tied to AstraZeneca account expansion.
**Effort:** Medium — leverages existing biomarker stratification; new outputs and regulatory templating layer.
