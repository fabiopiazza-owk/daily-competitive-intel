## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 Multi-Foundation-Model Pathology Ensemble (Meta-Encoder Integration)
**Rationale:** The meta-encoder paper demonstrates that orchestrating multiple pathology FMs outperforms single models. K Platform currently bets on specific FMs; adopting a meta-encoder approach would (a) de-risk foundation model selection, (b) create a defensible integration layer competitors can't easily replicate, and (c) directly address enterprise customer concerns about model vendor lock-in. This is existentially important given the Anthropic-Novartis alignment.
**Effort:** Medium — 6-8 weeks for POC. Requires benchmarking across 3+ pathology FMs (Virchow, UNI, Phikon) with existing K validation datasets.

### 🥈 LLM Provider Abstraction Layer for Agent Workflows
**Rationale:** With Anthropic now strategically aligned to Novartis and OpenAI rapidly maturing its Agents SDK, K Platform cannot afford single-provider dependency for its AI agent and reasoning capabilities. Build an abstraction layer that allows hot-swapping between Anthropic, OpenAI, and open-source models (Llama, Mixtral) for agent-driven workflows. This is both a technical hedge and an enterprise sales enabler ("we're model-agnostic, your data never touches a competitor's partner").
**Effort:** Medium — 4-6 weeks. Pattern exists in LangChain/LiteLLM; challenge is GxP-compliant logging and audit trails across providers.

### 🥉 Base-Editing & Novel Perturbation Screen Data Ingestion Pipeline
**Rationale:** Two Nature Biotech papers today on inducible base editing for cancer functional genomics signal that next-gen perturbation datasets will come in formats beyond standard CRISPR knockout screens. AstraZeneca and Sanofi are likely adopting these tools now. K Platform must be ready to ingest, normalize, and analyze base-editing screen outputs alongside existing data modalities to remain the preferred analytics platform.
**Effort:** Low-Medium — 3-4 weeks. Primarily data schema extension and validation pipeline work; model training comes later.
