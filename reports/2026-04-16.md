## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 GxP-Compliant Agentic Pipeline Framework (Leveraging OpenAI Agents SDK / Anthropic MCP)
**Rationale:** OpenAI's Agents SDK update and Anthropic's MCP are commoditizing agent orchestration. K Platform must build a thin GxP-compliance wrapper (audit trails, 21 CFR Part 11 logging, reproducibility guarantees) on top of one or both to enable automated pathology/genomics analysis workflows. This is the fastest path to "AI copilot for biostatisticians" — a top enterprise request — without reinventing the orchestration layer. Delay risks competitors (Biomni, NVIDIA Clara) shipping first.
**Effort:** ~6 weeks for a compliant wrapper + 2 integrations (Benchling ELN, internal LIMS); medium engineering effort.

### 🥈 Multimodal Fusion Benchmark Suite (Pathology + Genomics + Clinical)
**Rationale:** The multi-tissue multi-omics T1D paper and the gastric cancer digital biopsy paper both demonstrate that open research is converging on K Platform's core value prop. Without a published, reproducible benchmark showing K Platform's superiority in multimodal fusion, enterprise buyers (AstraZeneca, Sanofi) will face growing pressure to consider open-source alternatives. A benchmark suite also supports regulatory submissions.
**Effort:** ~4 weeks data curation + 3 weeks benchmarking; requires cross-team coordination (pathology, genomics, clinical data science).

### 🥉 Reverse-Translation Proteomics Data Connector
**Rationale:** Nature Biotech's reverse-translation peptide sequencing method could generate proteomics data at sequencing-scale throughput. Building an early data connector and normalization layer positions K Platform ahead of the curve when pharma partners begin generating this data type. First-mover advantage on a new modality reinforces the "multimodal AI platform" narrative.
**Effort:** ~3 weeks for connector + schema design; low engineering effort but requires proteomics domain expertise.
