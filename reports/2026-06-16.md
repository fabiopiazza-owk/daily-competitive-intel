## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 K Agent Evaluation Harness with Nature Meta-Analysis & Process-Level Benchmarks
**Rationale:** Deep research agent competition (Biomni, OpenAI) is rapidly maturing. Without rigorous, publishable benchmarks tied to pharma-relevant tasks (meta-analysis, evidence synthesis, process-level trace evaluation), K's agentic value proposition is hard to defend in enterprise procurement. Incorporating the Nature meta-analysis benchmark and WebStep-style semantic state tracking gives us defensible, customer-facing metrics.
**Effort:** Medium (6–8 weeks; reuse existing eval infra)

### 🥈 Spatial Biomarker Interpretability Module for Pathology Pipeline
**Rationale:** The interpretable semantic learning paper plus SCRIPT signal a market expectation that pathology-derived biomarkers come with native interpretability artifacts (regulatory- and KOL-ready). Hardening K's pathology stack with first-class interpretable spatial outputs strengthens AZ/Sanofi deal positioning and aligns with EU AI Act transparency requirements.
**Effort:** Medium-High (1 quarter; integrates with existing histo models)

### 🥉 Foundation Model Catalog: HiCFoundation Integration
**Rationale:** Genomics foundation models are proliferating. Establishing a curated K Platform "model catalog" pattern — starting with HiCFoundation for 3D genome / regulatory biomarker analysis — lets us add new modalities quickly without retraining and signals K as a platform-of-platforms vs. point solution.
**Effort:** Low-Medium (4–6 weeks for first model + catalog scaffold)
