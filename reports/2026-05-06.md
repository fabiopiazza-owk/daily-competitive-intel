## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 Clinical LLM Safety Evaluation Harness
**Rationale:** The arxiv scaling-laws paper proves accuracy ≠ safety in clinical LLMs. K needs a dedicated safety-evaluation harness (confident-wrong-rate, evidence-contradiction-rate, high-risk-error rate) baked into our model release process — this is both a regulatory shield (EU AI Act high-risk classification, FDA GMLP) and a sales differentiator vs. OpenAI/Anthropic generic models.
**Effort:** Medium (6–8 weeks; reuse existing eval infra, add safety-specific datasets and red-team tooling).

### 🥈 Tri-Modal Pathology+Clinical Contrastive Baseline (Haiku-style)
**Rationale:** Haiku validates the IF + histology + clinical contrastive recipe. K must reproduce internally with our pathology corpus, benchmark against Haiku, and publish — or risk being out-cited by an open competitor in the exact zone where AstraZeneca/Sanofi expect us to lead.
**Effort:** Large (10–12 weeks; data assembly is the critical path).

### 🥉 GxP-Readiness & Regulatory Whitepaper for Pharma R&D Agents
**Rationale:** GenAI-in-pharma regulatory reviews are now appearing in PubMed; FDA voucher-program turbulence (Sanofi/teplizumab) shows pharma is risk-sensitive about novel review/AI pathways. A K-branded regulatory readiness framework (GxP, EU AI Act high-risk, HIPAA, audit trails for agent actions) is a low-cost trust accelerator for enterprise procurement.
**Effort:** Small (3–4 weeks; cross-functional with Legal/Regulatory; mostly writing + customer interviews).
