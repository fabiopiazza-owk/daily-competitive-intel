## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 Cross-Cohort Robustness Benchmark & Public Leaderboard for Pathology Foundation Models
**Rationale:** The [breast cancer FM generalization paper](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42113320/) crystallizes the #1 customer objection to K's pathology stack. Publishing a TCGA-vs-external-cohort leaderboard with K's MOSAIC results — and a customer-runnable harness — converts a vulnerability into a differentiator and pre-empts the same critique from OpenAI/Clara entrants.
**Effort:** Medium (6–8 weeks; reuse existing eval infra, 3–4 external cohorts, 1 paper).

### 🥈 Calibrated Uncertainty Layer (Conformal Prediction) Across K Navigator Outputs
**Rationale:** [Conformal Path Reasoning](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08077v1) and [GRAPHLCP](http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08074v1) show conformal methods now extend cleanly to graphs and KG retrieval — the exact substrates K uses for biomarker and target reasoning. Coverage guarantees are a concrete answer to regulators and medical affairs reviewers, and a differentiator vs. OpenAI DeployCo's general-purpose stack.
**Effort:** Medium (one quarter; wrap existing inference endpoints with conformal calibration sets per use case).

### 🥉 K-as-SKILL.md: Portable Capability Packages + MCP Server
**Rationale:** [SkCC](https://huggingface.co/papers/2605.03353) confirms SKILL.md is winning as the agent-capability standard, and Anthropic MCP is the dominant interop bus. Exposing K's pathology, trial-matching, and biomarker tools as SKILL.md + MCP makes K the "default biomedical capability layer" inside any pharma agent stack (Biomni, internal AstraZeneca agents, DeployCo deployments) rather than a competing platform.
**Effort:** Small–Medium (4–6 weeks; thin adapter layer over existing K APIs + reference Claude/Biomni integration).
