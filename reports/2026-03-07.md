## 3. Technical Frontier

- **[Mozi — Governed Drug Discovery Agents](https://huggingface.co/papers/2603.03655)** — Most relevant paper today. Introduces dependency-aware tool governance for LLM agents in drug discovery. Key idea: constrain tool invocation with a directed acyclic graph of allowed workflows, with checkpoint validation. Directly applicable to K Platform agent architecture.
- **[RAG for Regulatory Compliance of Drug Information & Clinical Trial Protocols](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41709726/)** — RAG system purpose-built for evaluating regulatory compliance of clinical trial protocols and drug labeling. Relevant for K's clinical trial optimization module — could be integrated as a compliance-checking agent within trial design workflows.
- **[RoentMod: Synthetic CXR Modification to Identify Model Shortcuts](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41792409/)** — Addresses shortcut learning in medical imaging models via synthetic image modification. Directly relevant to K's pathology AI model validation and bias detection. Useful methodology for demonstrating model robustness to pharma clients and regulators.
- **[MASQuant: Modality-Aware Smoothing Quantization for MLLMs](https://huggingface.co/papers/2603.04800)** — Quantization technique that handles modality-specific activation distributions in multimodal LLMs. K Platform runs multimodal (pathology + genomics + clinical) models; this could reduce inference costs significantly while maintaining accuracy.
- **[KARL: Knowledge Agents via Reinforcement Learning](https://huggingface.co/papers/2603.05218)** — RL-trained enterprise search agents with a benchmark suite. The approach of training search agents with RL on domain-specific corpora maps to K Platform's need for intelligent retrieval across clinical trial databases and literature.
