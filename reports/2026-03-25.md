## 3. Technical Frontier

- **[MedObvious: Medical Moravec's Paradox in VLMs](http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23501v1)** — Shows that VLMs fail basic pre-diagnostic visual sanity checks (laterality, image orientation) despite generating fluent reports. **K relevance:** Critical for any K Platform module using VLMs for pathology QC or report generation. This paper should inform our validation/safety gating for AI-assisted pathology — we must test for "obvious" failures, not just diagnostic accuracy.

- **[DIAMOND DeepClust: Clustering the protein universe](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-026-03030-z)** (Nature Methods) — Ultra-fast protein clustering at low sequence identity for large-scale protein organization. **K relevance:** Relevant to K's target/drug discovery modules. Could enable faster protein family analysis in multi-omics pipelines. Evaluate for integration into genomics feature engineering.

- **[Deep learning integration of alternative fragmentation in LC-MS](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-026-03042-9)** (Nature Methods) — Single DL model unifies collision-, electron-, and photon-based fragmentation for enhanced proteome coverage. **K relevance:** Proteomics data is increasingly important for biomarker discovery. This technique could boost signal quality from proteomic inputs into K's multimodal models.

- **[AgentSLR: Automating Systematic Literature Reviews](https://huggingface.co/papers/2603.22327)** — LLM-based agent automates the complete SLR workflow from retrieval to synthesis in epidemiology. **K relevance:** Directly applicable to K's clinical evidence synthesis features. Could inform an "evidence review agent" module for trial design or regulatory submission support.

- **[Regulating AI Agents](http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23471v1)** — Comprehensive framework for regulating autonomous AI systems. **K relevance:** As K Platform moves toward agentic workflows (automated biomarker discovery, trial optimization agents), this paper maps the regulatory landscape we'll need to navigate for EU AI Act compliance and FDA Software-as-Medical-Device (SaMD) classification.
