## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 In-Context MIL for low-label pathology onboarding
**Rationale:** New indications and rare disease trials are K's stickiest enterprise use cases, and labeled WSI data is always the bottleneck. ICL-MIL could let us onboard a new AstraZeneca indication in days instead of weeks, directly attacking Phylo's positioning. ([paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.06458))
**Effort:** Medium — 1 ML engineer, 4-6 weeks for a pilot on existing pathology bag.

### 🥈 Biosecurity & agent-safety evaluation harness aligned to ABC-Bench
**Rationale:** Before K ships any agentic biology feature to Sanofi/AstraZeneca compliance review, we need a documented red-team and benchmark report. This becomes a procurement differentiator and pre-empts EU AI Act high-risk classification battles. ([ABC-Bench](http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11150v1))
**Effort:** Medium-High — joint security + ML, 6-8 weeks; reusable as a sales artifact.

### 🥉 Temporal-leakage audit + test-time adaptation layer for clinical models
**Rationale:** The STAT sepsis piece and U-TTT paper together signal that pharma and regulators will demand both (a) proof of no temporal leakage and (b) graceful site-shift handling. Bundling these into a single "Clinical Robustness Report" gives our enterprise customers something to show their internal AI governance boards. ([STAT](https://www.statnews.com/2026/06/10/sepsis-algorithm-shouldnt-require-time-machine-ai-prognosis/) | [U-TTT](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.11032))
**Effort:** Low-Medium — 1 ML + 1 clinical lead, 3-4 weeks; mostly documentation + lightweight TTA wrapper.
