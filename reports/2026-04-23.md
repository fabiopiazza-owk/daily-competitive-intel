- **K implication:** If validated, this technique could dramatically reduce the cost of deploying Owkin's pathology models across new hospital sites and trial arms — a persistent pain point in enterprise deals. R&D team should evaluate for integration into the MOSAIC/pathology pipeline.
- [Arxiv: 2604.20824](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20824v1)

### 🟡 Nature Biotechnology: "Deeper Is Not Always Better in Plasma Proteomics"
Commentary arguing that increasing proteomics depth doesn't linearly improve clinical utility — robustness and bias matter more. Directly relevant to K Platform's multi-omics biomarker discovery capabilities where clients often demand maximum feature depth.
- **K implication:** Strengthens the case for K Platform's curation-first, interpretable biomarker approach over brute-force deep proteomics. Should be cited in enterprise pitch decks to counter "more data = better" objections from pharma data science leads.

## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 In-Context Batch Effect Normalization for Cross-Site Pathology Deployment
**Rationale:** The "Closing the Domain Gap" paper (Arxiv 2604.20824) presents a practical, zero-retraining approach to batch effect correction using control samples at inference time. This is the single biggest friction point in scaling K Platform's pathology models to new trial sites and hospital partners. Implementing this could cut deployment timelines from weeks to hours and directly unblock stalled enterprise expansions with AZ and Sanofi.
**Effort:** Medium (2-3 sprints). Requires validation on internal WSI datasets, integration into inference pipeline, and QA against existing normalization approaches.

### 🥈 PII Redaction / Privacy Layer Parity with OpenAI Privacy Filter
**Rationale:** OpenAI just open-sourced a state-of-the-art PII detection model. Every pharma procurement conversation will now benchmark against it. K Platform needs either to integrate this model (it's open-weight) or demonstrate equivalent/superior privacy guarantees in our data governance layer. This is table-stakes for GxP and HIPAA compliance storytelling.
**Effort:** Low-Medium (1-2 sprints). Evaluate OpenAI's model on K Platform data types (clinical notes, pathology reports, genomic metadata), integrate or build a wrapper, and update compliance documentation.

### 🥉 Scientific Reasoning Guardrails for LLM-Assisted Hypothesis Generation
**Rationale:** The "AI Scientists Produce Results Without Reasoning Scientifically" paper (HF 2604.18805) is a reputational landmine. If K Platform's AI-generated biomarker hypotheses or literature insights are shown to lack scientific rigor, it undermines trust with pharma R&D leads. We need structured epistemic checks (causal reasoning validation, reproducibility flags, uncertainty quantification) in any LLM-in-the-loop feature.
**Effort:** Medium (2-3 sprints). Requires defining a scientific reasoning evaluation framework, implementing automated checks, and integrating into the hypothesis generation UX with clear provenance indicators.
