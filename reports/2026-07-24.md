## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 Pathology-report LLM risk stratification module (K-Path Reports)
**Rationale:** SCRIPT and similar academic pipelines show pharma will expect LLM-driven stratification from unstructured path reports as table stakes. K owns the pathology narrative; combining WSI foundation models with report-level LLM stratification is a defensible bundle no competitor can replicate quickly. Also directly addresses AstraZeneca's oncology biomarker workflow asks.
**Effort:** Medium (6–8 weeks) — leverage existing pathology FM + integrate a report-tuned LLM with counterfactual-necessity explanations for regulatory readiness.

### 🥈 Spatial transcriptomics fusion layer for I/O biomarker discovery
**Rationale:** Two Nature papers this week (Fullscope-seq, neoantigen-T cell colocalization) confirm spatial multi-omics is now the reference substrate for I/O biomarker programs. K's multimodal fusion story is incomplete without a spatial transcriptomics ingestion + colocalization analytics module. Sanofi and AZ I/O teams will ask within two quarters.
**Effort:** Large (1 quarter+) — new data connector, alignment with pathology FM embeddings, benchmarks against Spatialproteomics.

### 🥉 Regulatory-grade explanation layer using counterfactual necessity
**Rationale:** The arxiv paper on counterfactual necessity for time-series explanation and the RCT on responsibility allocation in AI-assisted diagnosis both point to a hardening regulatory expectation: sufficiency-based explanations aren't enough. Building this into K's trial-optimization and biomarker outputs is a differentiator vs. Benchling/Phylo, and pre-empts EU AI Act high-risk auditability requirements.
**Effort:** Small–Medium (4–6 weeks) — research spike + integration into existing explainability API surface.
