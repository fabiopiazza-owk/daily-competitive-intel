**Rationale:** LLM-as-a-Verifier (arXiv 2607.05391) plus regulatory pressure (EU AI Act, biosignals-safety paper) make an auditable, model-based verification layer table stakes. K agents that produce biomarker calls, trial-eligibility decisions, or literature syntheses must ship with an independent verifier trail — this is both a compliance moat and a differentiator vs. Biomni.
**Effort:** M (6–8 weeks) — leverage existing eval infra; wrap agent outputs with a verifier ensemble and log provenance.

### 🥈 Synthetic 3D Medical Imaging Pipeline (Chest-CT first)
**Rationale:** CONFLUX shows controllable 3D CT generation with clinical attributes is production-viable. K can offer pharma partners synthetic cohorts for rare indications, augmenting scarce imaging trial data — a direct win for AstraZeneca oncology and Sanofi rare disease conversations, and a hedge against Nvidia Clara commoditizing this capability.
**Effort:** L (1 quarter) — needs RL post-training infra, radiologist-in-the-loop QA, and a governance/consent story for synthetic-data provenance.

### 🥉 KG-Augmented Reasoning Module for Biomarker & Rare Disease Workflows
**Rationale:** KGRD (pediatric rare genetic disorders) validates that KG-grounded LLM reasoning outperforms pure LLM for regulated diagnostic-adjacent tasks. K should ship a KG-reasoning module tied to its multimodal biomarker outputs — turning opaque predictions into auditable, citable reasoning chains that survive pharma medical-affairs review.
**Effort:** M–L (10–12 weeks) — build on existing ontology assets (OMIM, MONDO, GO); wire into agent orchestrator.
