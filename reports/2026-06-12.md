## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 Provenance & audit layer for K agent outputs aligned to EU Code of Practice
**Rationale:** OpenAI just publicly anchored to the EU Code; AZ/Sanofi procurement teams will demand parity within the quarter. Every model call in K (pathology FM, genomics, agent reasoning step) should emit signed provenance metadata (model version, training data lineage, confidence, GxP-relevant flags). Also closes the Cold-Start Safety Gap by formalizing session-init checks.
**Effort:** Medium — 1 quarter, mostly platform/infra + legal review, no new ML.

### 🥈 Pathology FM + Sparse Autoencoder interpretability module
**Rationale:** Two converging papers this week (semantic biomarker learning + compositional SAE histology representations) point to SAEs becoming the standard explainability layer for pathology FMs. Shipping this on K's H-optimus / MOSAIC stack gives us a regulator-ready story and a concrete differentiator vs. NVIDIA/Abridge (who are focused on clinical conversation, not tissue).
**Effort:** Medium-high — 1 quarter of applied research + 1 quarter productization.

### 🥉 MCP-compliant tool catalog for K (pathology, genomics, trial design endpoints)
**Rationale:** Multiple signals (Evoflux, ToolSense, HyperTool, OpenAI Ona) confirm MCP-style agent workflows are crystallizing as the enterprise default. If K publishes a clean MCP tool surface, pharma data-science teams can drive K from Claude/OpenAI/internal agents — making K the "tool layer" rather than competing as a full agent. Defensive move against OpenAI's enterprise expansion.
**Effort:** Low-medium — 4–6 weeks for v1 catalog + auth/schema; iterate from there.
