## 1. Top 3 Signals Today

### 🔴 HistoAtlas: Pan-Cancer Morphology Atlas Linking Histomics to Molecular Programs
A new paper presents [HistoAtlas](https://huggingface.co/papers/2603.16587), extracting 38 interpretable histomic features from 6,745 H&E slides across 21 TCGA cancer types, systematically linking morphology to survival, gene expression, somatic mutations, and immune subtypes. **K Platform implication:** This is squarely in Owkin's wheelhouse — a direct academic competitor/validator for our multimodal pathology-to-genomics linking. If HistoAtlas open-sources features or pre-trained models, it could commoditize capabilities we sell. We should evaluate integration into K Platform's feature library and ensure our proprietary models (particularly on non-TCGA, pharma-partnered cohorts) maintain a differentiation moat. Consider a rapid benchmarking sprint against HistoAtlas features.

### 🔴 ARPA-H Developing FDA-Authorized AI Agents for Clinical Deployment
[STAT reports](https://www.statnews.com/2026/03/18/how-arpah-developing-fda-authorized-ai-tools-ai-prognosis/?utm_campaign=rss) that ARPA-H is actively building AI agents designed for FDA authorization and tested within clinical trials. **K Platform implication:** This establishes a concrete regulatory pathway for autonomous AI agents in healthcare — not just decision-support but agentic systems. For K Platform's clinical trial optimization modules, this signals that FDA is moving toward formal frameworks for AI-in-the-loop trial designs. We should proactively engage ARPA-H's framework to position K Platform's trial analytics as "agent-ready" and GxP-compliant ahead of competitors.

### 🟡 CellVoyager: Autonomous AI Agent for Single-Cell Biology Discovery
[Nature Methods published CellVoyager](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41592-026-03029-6), an AI agent that autonomously explores scRNA-seq datasets and generates novel biological hypotheses. **K Platform implication:** Autonomous biological discovery agents represent the next frontier beyond our current supervised analysis workflows. This validates the product thesis for an "AI biologist" layer atop K Platform's multimodal data. If we don't build agentic exploration capabilities into K Platform, tools like CellVoyager will become the interface pharma scientists prefer, relegating K to a backend data platform.

Directly applicable to K Platform's multimodal fusion engine — could improve how we combine pathology, genomics, and clinical evidence with principled uncertainty quantification.

- **[DILIConsult](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41826786/)** — Multi-agent LLM framework for evaluating drug-induced liver injury in ICU settings. Demonstrates clinical-grade multi-agent safety reasoning. Relevant architecture pattern for K Platform's drug safety signal modules.

- **[ViT-AdaLA](https://huggingface.co/papers/2603.16063)** — Adapting Vision Transformers with linear attention, reducing quadratic complexity. Applicable to K Platform's whole-slide image processing where sequence length (patch count) is a persistent bottleneck.

## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 **P0: Agentic Biological Discovery Module**
**Rationale:** CellVoyager (Nature Methods), NVIDIA's agentic AI push, and ARPA-H's FDA-authorized agents all converge on one thesis: pharma R&D workflows are going agentic. K Platform needs an "AI Biologist" layer — an agent that autonomously explores multimodal datasets (pathology + genomics + clinical), generates hypotheses, and presents findings with uncertainty quantification. Without this, K risks becoming a passive data platform while CellVoyager-like tools own the scientist-facing interface. **Leverage:** LPF framework for multi-evidence reasoning; GPT-5.4 nano for cost-efficient sub-agents.

### 🥈 **P1: HistoAtlas Feature Benchmarking & Integration**
**Rationale:** HistoAtlas's 38 interpretable histomic features across 21 cancer types set a new public benchmark for computational pathology. K Platform must (a) benchmark our foundation models against HistoAtlas on TCGA to quantify our advantage, (b) evaluate integrating their open features as a baseline layer, and (c) demonstrate superiority on proprietary non-TCGA cohorts to protect competitive positioning in AstraZeneca/Sanofi deals. **Timeline:** 2-week sprint; results feed into Q2 enterprise pitch decks.

### 🥉 **P2: Linear Attention Adaptation for Whole-Slide Image Processing**
**Rationale:** ViT-AdaLA demonstrates that existing ViT foundation models can be adapted to linear attention without retraining from scratch — reducing quadratic complexity. For K Platform, WSI processing is a compute bottleneck (10,000+ patches per slide). Adapting our pathology ViTs with linear attention could cut inference costs 40-60% and enable real-time analysis in clinical trial reads. **Leverage:** Direct paper methodology; low engineering lift if our models are ViT-based.
