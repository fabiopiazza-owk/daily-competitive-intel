## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 K-Bench: a LifeSciBench-style internal + public evaluation suite for pathology, biomarker, and trial-design tasks
**Rationale:** OpenAI's LifeSciBench will become the default reference. We need (a) our own pharma-relevant benchmark with multimodal tasks LifeSciBench under-covers (WSI + omics + EHR), and (b) public K results on LifeSciBench itself. Without this, every deal review starts on OpenAI's terms.
**Effort:** Large — 1 quarter, 2 ML engineers + 1 clinical scientist + benchmark curation contractor.

### 🥈 Pathology-report → risk-stratification module with SCRIPT as baseline
**Rationale:** SCRIPT is a near-identical capability published openly; we must demonstrate K's multimodal (report + WSI + molecular) version beats text-only baselines on at least two indications before pharma evaluators cite it.
**Effort:** Medium — 6–8 weeks leveraging existing K pathology pipeline + report ingestion.

### 🥉 GxP-grade agent control plane: certificate-bound tool authority + structured ledger state
**Rationale:** Combining the Sovereign Execution Brokers and LedgerAgent patterns with DeepMind's AI Control Roadmap framing gives K a defensible, auditable agent layer that maps cleanly to GxP and EU AI Act high-risk requirements — a differentiator vs. OpenAI/Anthropic horizontal agents.
**Effort:** Medium-Large — 8–10 weeks; coordinate with security and regulatory affairs.
