## 6. Build Signals for K Platform

### 🥇 Domain-Specific Latent Autoencoders for Pathology Modalities
**Rationale:** Today's HuggingFace paper provides controlled evidence that swapping in a domain-trained VAE yields larger quality gains than any diffusion architecture change. K's pathology image pipeline currently relies on general-purpose encoders. Training dedicated latent spaces for H&E, IHC, and mIF would improve feature extraction for all downstream tasks (biomarker discovery, treatment response prediction) and create a defensible moat. This is empirically validated and directly on our critical path.
**Effort:** ~8-10 engineering weeks (dataset curation 3w, VAE training 3w, integration + benchmarking 2-4w). Requires pathology image corpus already in-house.

### 🥈 CRISPR/Base-Editing Screen Data Ingestion Pipeline
**Rationale:** Two Nature Biotech papers today confirm inducible base editing is becoming the standard for in vivo functional genomics screens. The volume of perturbation–phenotype datasets will explode. K Platform needs a first-class connector for base-editing screen outputs (sgRNA libraries, editing efficiency, phenotype readouts) to train next-gen target ID and biomarker models. Critical for Sanofi/AZ functional genomics collaborations.
**Effort:** ~5-6 engineering weeks (schema design 1w, parser/ETL 2w, integration with existing genomics module 2-3w).

### 🥉 Anthropic MCP Connector with Pharma-Grade Audit Trail
**Rationale:** Novartis CEO on Anthropic's board makes Claude the most pharma-politically relevant LLM. K must offer a production-grade MCP (Model Context Protocol) connector allowing Claude to query K Platform's datasets and models with full GxP audit logging, RBAC, and provenance tracking. This positions K as the "data & model layer" that Anthropic plugs into rather than a competitor to be displaced.
**Effort:** ~6-8 engineering weeks (MCP spec implementation 2w, audit/GxP logging 2-3w, auth/RBAC 1-2w, testing + pharma validation 1-2w).
